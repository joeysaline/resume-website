About Me

My name is Joey Saline and I am currently enrolled at the University at Albany. This year I am going into my senior year, which means I have been lifting weights for roughly 7 years. I first touched the weight room my freshman year of high school at the school gym. Although I did not know what I was doing at the time, I always felt better leaving than I did entering. This feeling has yet to subside.

I went on and off throughout high school, but the important part was how interested I was in learning about different ways to achieve better results. I spent most of my free time watching YouTube and collecting information on how to properly do different exercises, as well as how to better structure my routine and diet.

By the time I was a sophomore in college, I felt like I knew everything there was to know about training and nutrition. I encouraged two of my close friends who are two years younger to go to the gym and experience all the benefits waiting for them. So, I took it upon myself to coach them, and after just a month and a half they both looked considerably different from where they started.

During this time period I would workout alongside them as they would follow my training routine, and I would demonstrate proper form for each exercise. Constantly I was unleashing the knowledge I gathered over many years, and informing them of every miniscule aspect that could make a difference.

Now, I am proud to say they both surpassed me and maintain much better consistency than I do while away at college. At this point my lifestyle is nowhere near healthy while I am away at college, so my knowledge definetely surpasses my physical appearance. This website will showcase different services that I could offer as a personal coach if I had decided to pursue this career path.

Aspirations

My priorites have shifted over the years and I am becoming a lot more health conscious. I realized that my body is very injury prone, or at least I would push myself to the point of injury, and I aim to slowly and more carefully progress to my goals. I also find that taking in sufficient nutrients through my diet is crucial to solving my problem of getting sick every other week. I am known to be the first one of my friends to get sick, and then spread that sickness around. Ensuring my immune system stays healthy is now another of my priorites.

For lifting, I stopped caring about what I look as much, and now I really want to see my limits when it comes to strength. I never intentionally tried to get stronger on traditional powerlifting lifts before. My numbers always sat dormant even though I would train consistently. Once I hit 225 lbs on bench around 2 years ago, I never progressed to anything substantially heavier. My current heaviest bench press is 245 lbs, heaviest squat is 325 lbs, and deadlift is just 385 lbs. I am aiming to bring my squat up to 405 lbs, my bench to 315 lbs, and my deadlift to 495 lbs. I believe I could accomplish all of that within a year.

I hope to continue my lifting journey well into my life after I settle into my career. As a Computer Science major, I do not see much activity in my future, so I am determined to maintain a healthy lifestyle and continue to set new goals down the line.
